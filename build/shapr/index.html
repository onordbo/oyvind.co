Shapr

Your personal fitness coach.

Everywhere.

Note:

App Concept, User Interface Design, Wearable Technology

This is the creative task for my Digital Media Creative admission to Hyper Island, and it is currently only a concept. The task was to create a digital solution/service/product that were set ten years into the future.

Problem:

I had this problem when I started working out, five months ago. I didn’t know anything about exercising right, or fitness in general. I just gambled about in the gym on the machines, assuming that if I wore myself out every time, I would just naturally progress. And in essence you can, but it’s not very effective, and if you're doing it wrong, you can hurt yourself. A lot.

The world is already filled with a vast amount of wearable fitness technology, wristbands, apps, dongles, etc. They do all sorts of amazing things and track all sorts of data. But what good does that data do if you don’t understand it?

Solution:

Introducing Shapr, a smart rubberband that tracks all your vitals. It's the personal coach that knows everything about your health, that can always tell you what to do to progress efficiently. It’s constantly monitoring your health, and translates that into data you can understand, so you can focus on the important parts; like actually going to the gym.

Shapr tracks data like running speed, heart rate, oxygen saturation, calories burned etc, etc, just like everything else. But what it then does is crunch all that data into a format that anyone can understand.

I don’t how many push-ups I should do when I work out, but my body does! Sadly, it doesn’t talk to me. Shapr aims to deliver a fully autonomous workout program, that simply tells you what to do, based on your current health. No research required.

Frictionless, automated calendars:

What’s really great about Shapr, is that you don’t have to worry about planning your workout schedule. It’s fully automated, and if you skip a workout, Shapr will simply recalibrate what you’ll do for next session.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the possibilities for increased life expectancy with Shapr could be amazing. Real-time lifestyle feedback paired with simple digestible information might just be the next big thing to nudge people into a more healthy lifestyle.
